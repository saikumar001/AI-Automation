# LinkedIn Post Best Practices

This reference provides guidance for creating effective LinkedIn posts.

## Content Guidelines

### Length
- **Optimal**: 300-1300 characters for maximum engagement
- **Maximum**: 3000 characters (LinkedIn truncates beyond this in feed)
- Short, punchy posts (1-3 short paragraphs) perform better than walls of text

### First Line Hook
The first 1-2 lines appear in the LinkedIn feed preview. Make them compelling to drive clicks:
- Ask a provocative question
- State a surprising fact
- Promise clear value
- Example: "I spent 3 years optimizing my morning routine. Here's what actually works:"

### Formatting
- Use line breaks between ideas (not dense paragraphs)
- Emojis sparingly: 1-3 max, placed at beginning or end of post, not mid-sentence
- Avoid ALL CAPS (looks like shouting)
- Use bold/italics via LinkedIn's formatting? LinkedIn supports limited formatting in mobile app only; better to keep plain text
- Lists: Use numbered or bullet points (supported in LinkedIn's native formatting)

### Hashtags
- **Number**: 3-5 hashtags is optimal
- **Placement**: At the END of the post (before any @mentions)
- **Mix**:
  - 1-2 broad (#technology, #business)
  - 1-2 niche (#PythonAsync, #DevOps)
  - 1 branded if company/event specific (#TechConf2026)
- **Research**: Check hashtag follower counts in LinkedIn search before using
- Avoid overused spammy hashtags like #followforfollow

### Mentions (@)
- Use `@` to tag companies, people, or events when relevant
- Mentioning others increases reach but do so genuinely
- Example: "Presented at @Google Cloud Next 2026" (if true)
- Don't mention random influencers just for reach

### Links
- LinkedIn auto-generates link previews (image + title + description)
- Use short, clean URLs (LinkedIn展示不是用bitly的链接)
- Place links at the END if you want to avoid preview truncation, or embed naturally if you want the preview to show
- For multiple links, separate with spaces or line breaks

### Call-to-Action (CTA)
End with a question or CTA to drive engagement:
- "What's your experience with this?"
- "Agree or disagree?"
- "Share your thoughts in the comments"
- "DM me if interested"
- "Repost to spread the word"

Avoid direct sales CTAs ("Buy now", "Sign up here") unless it's promotional content where that's expected.

### Tone & Professionalism
- LinkedIn is professional but not corporate-stiff
- Share personal stories, lessons learned, failures
- Vulnerability and humility perform well
- Avoid overly promotional content unless balanced with value
- First person ("I", "my") is fine; second person ("you") is engaging

## Media Attachments

### Images
- **Recommended size**: 1200 x 627 pixels (1.91:1 ratio) for link previews
- **Square posts**: 1080 x 1080 pixels
- **File size**: Under 5MB
- **Format**: JPG, PNG (JPG smaller)
- **Multiple images**: Up to 9 images in a single post (LinkedIn supports carousel)
- **Text overlay**: Keep minimal; many users disable images; post text must standalone

### Videos
- **Length**: Short (under 10 minutes), but 1-3 minutes ideal
- **Size**: Up to 5GB (but keep it small for faster upload)
- **Format**: MP4, MOV
- **Captions**: Add subtitles; many watch without sound
- **First frame**: Choose compelling thumbnail

### Documents (LinkedIn Articles)
- For posts over 3000 chars, consider publishing as a LinkedIn Article instead
- Articles support formatting, images, subheadings
- Articles get separate URL and can be shared as regular post with link

## Timing (When to Post)

**General best times** (based on Buffer/Social Media studies):
- Weekdays 8-10am local time (people checking LinkedIn before work)
- Weekdays 12-2pm (lunch break)
- Weekdays 4-6pm (end of work day)
- **Avoid**: Weekends, holidays, late nights

**But**: Test your own audience using LinkedIn analytics. Your followers' time zones matter.

## What to Avoid

- **Overposting**: 1-3 posts per week is plenty; daily posting can feel spammy
- **Hashtag stuffing**: More than 7 hashtags reduces engagement
- **Inflammatory politics/controversy**: LinkedIn is professional; controversial takes should be thoughtful, not trollish
- **Duplicate content**: Don't copy-paste same post repeatedly
- **Misleading claims**: LinkedIn can fact-check or remove posts with false claims
- **Engagement bait**: "Like if you agree!" or "Comment YES/NO" - LinkedIn algorithm may demote

## Post Types & Templates

### Achievement Announcement
```
[Proud/excited] [milestone] achieved!

[1-2 sentences of context or story]

[What it means / what's next]

[Gratitude: tag/thank people involved]

[Relevant hashtags]
```

### Industry Insight
```
[Hot take/question] about [industry topic]

[Evidence or personal experience supporting claim]

[Why it matters to your audience]

[Expertise positioning: "As a [role], I've seen..."]

[Provocative question to spark comments]

[Hashtags]
```

### Job Change
```
Thrilled to announce I've joined [Company] as [Role]!

[Why excited: mission/tech/culture]

[What I'll be working on]

[Looking forward to connecting with team]

#newjob #companyname #hiring
```

### Event Recap
```
Just attended/put on [Event Name]. Here are my key takeaways:

1. [Takeaway 1]
2. [Takeaway 2]
3. [Takeaway 3]

[Most surprising insight]

[Shout-out to organizers/speakers]

[Event hashtag]
```

## Post Performance Indicators

- **Engagement rate**: (Likes + Comments + Shares + Clicks) / Impressions
- Good: 2-3%+
- Average: 1-2%
- Poor: <1%

When testing, compare posts of similar type; don't compare a poll to a plain text post.

---

**Remember**: LinkedIn's algorithm favors posts that generate meaningful interactions (comments, especially longer ones). Ask questions to encourage discussion.
